## Novelty audit — verdicts per claimed contribution

The four contributions of §1.4 and the eight-practice integration of §10 were cross-referenced against `docs/sources.md` clusters A–O.

1. **§1.4 contribution 1 — *definition of the effort gap and an operationalisation as measurable KPIs*.** Verdict: **incremental**. The "effort gap" naming is novel as far as `docs/sources.md` records (no comparable source found in clusters A, B, D); the underlying *idea* — that LLM assistance reduces decompilation effort — is established by L-RE-1, L-RE-2, L-RE-3, and qualified by L-RE-4. Framing in §1.4 does not currently differentiate the contribution from L-RE-2 (Hu et al., 2024), which already reports a 24.4 % cognitive-burden reduction. **Action**: address via RDB-12.
2. **§1.4 contribution 2 — *two empirical case studies with complete artifact provenance*.** Verdict: **novel**. No comparable source found in `docs/sources.md` for AI-assisted RE case studies vendored with full transcript/provenance/commit-pinning. The closest comparator is L-IOTAPP-1 (IoTFlow, Schmidt et al., 2023, 9,889 apps statically analysed), which is corpus-scale rather than per-case and does not preserve a transcript. Verdict supported by absence of prior art in clusters A, D, O.
3. **§1.4 contribution 3 — *auditable methodology that treats AI conversation transcripts as research artifacts*.** Verdict: **incremental, framing defect**. The transcript-as-artifact discipline overlaps with L-SLOP-10 (Cheng et al., 2025, ethical-use guidelines) and L-SLOP-7 (Stockholm Declaration), and the artifact-level provenance overlaps with L-MC-3 / L-MC-4 (mixing real and synthetic). The §1.4 framing does not currently contrast against any of these. **Action**: address via RDB-02 and RDB-12.
4. **§1.4 contribution 4 — *synthesis of interoperability gains versus dual-use risks*.** Verdict: **incremental**. The dual-use framing is well-established (L-VD-4, L-VD-5, L-COUNTER-1..6); the §1.4 synthesis is incremental in scope (consumer-IoT case studies + recursive meta-case) rather than novel in framing. The §1.4 wording does not currently differentiate.
5. **§10 eight-practice integration claim — *"the novelty is the integration"*.** Verdict: **unsupported-novelty as currently framed**. The disclaimer "[n]one of these practices is individually novel" is correct, but the claim that *the integration* is novel is not contrasted against any specific comparator in `docs/sources.md`. Closest comparators are L-SLOP-7, L-SLOP-10, L-SLOP-12 (cluster I governance and disclosure proposals) and L-MC-3, L-MC-4 (cluster J provenance and data-mixing for foundation-model training). **Action**: address via RDB-02.
6. **§10 fourth structural claim — *democratisation of science production via AI-assistance*.** Verdict: **novel framing, no comparable source found**. The classical-citizen-science-versus-AI-assisted-citizen-science contrast (`main.md:639`) does not have a direct comparator in `docs/sources.md`; closest indirect comparators are L-SLOP-5 (Kendall & Teixeira da Silva, 2023, system-level concerns) and L-SLOP-8 (Suchak et al., 2025, NHANES paper-mill output). The contrast is narrowly novel because no source equates the *democratisation* idea to citizen science specifically. The framing is internally consistent with §7.6 / §7.7 and does not require a new contrast paragraph; flagged as the most defensible novelty in the paper.

**RDB-01 — quadruple repetition of the L-SLOP-1/2/4 fabricated-citation statistics across §5.6, §7.6, §9.4, and §10.** This is the single defect whose remediation most improves the reader's experience: it removes the strongest *recap* impression in the paper and frees §10 to do the rhetorical work it is structured for.
