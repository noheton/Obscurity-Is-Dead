Methodology

The four-stage research pipeline, the verification-status ladder with its PRISMA 2020 / GRADE crosswalk (added 2026-05-05), the ten-stage agent pipeline that produces the paper, and the redaction discipline that runs alongside everything else.

The four-stage research method

  1. Acquire. Vendor APKs / firmware / community implementations are acquired and embedded under experiments/<case>/original/ at a pinned commit (e.g. ffdf60c). The pin is recorded in experiments/<case>/provenance.md.
  2. Analyse. AI-assisted analysis produces a protocol map, surface enumeration, or weakness table. Every conversation is preserved verbatim under experiments/<case>/raw_conversations*/ (Practice 1).
  3. Audit. Every claim from the analysis pass is audited against the embedded code at the pinned commit. The pinned commit is the ground truth; the AI's output is a claim to be checked, not authority.
  4. Validate. A smoke test re-runs against the live device (or, in the meta-process case, against the rebuilt PDF + the rule-12 mirror parity check). A feedback loop from validation back into analysis is treated as a first-class transition rather than as an exception path — tooling drift means an identical prompt at a later date will not produce identical output, and the workflow, not the model's tokens, is the reproducible unit.

Source: paper/main.md §2 / paper/main-condensed.md §3; docs/methodology.md.

Verification-status ladder ↔ PRISMA 2020 / GRADE crosswalk

Every cited source carries exactly one ladder rung. A paper claim may invoke a source at its current rung or below; load-bearing or contested claims still require [lit-read]. As of 2026-05-05 the project ladder is explicitly aligned with two community vocabularies along two distinct axes:

  • Retrieval-depth axis ↔ PRISMA 2020 flow phases (Identification → Screening → Eligibility / Included). Resolves which stage of the systematic-review funnel the entry has reached.
  • Invocation-strength axis ↔ GRADE certainty of evidence (High / Moderate / Low / Very Low). Resolves how strongly a paper claim may invoke the entry once cited.

Two notes that matter for downstream readers:

  1. GRADE certainty rates the underlying evidence, not the retrieval procedure. A [lit-read] entry that reports a small observational study still rates Low under GRADE. The mapping above is the floor a rung permits, not the ceiling; the writer's invocation-floor judgement still gates load-bearing claims on a human read regardless of GRADE.
  2. The two AI-assisted rungs collapse PRISMA's Eligibility and Inclusion phases. Under our process, eligibility and inclusion-for-citation are decided by the same act (the agent or the human reads the source and decides whether to invoke it). A future review-style adoption of this ladder would split the two phases back out.

The redaction discipline

A redaction discipline runs alongside the ladder and is treated as a precondition rather than as an afterthought. Several maintainer handles, repository paths, device serial numbers, and credentials are redacted as [REDACTED:<type>:<id>] markers in every researcher-authored file; the full register, the audit trail, and the git-history-rewrite plan that purges raw values from prior commits all live in docs/redaction-policy.md, docs/redaction-audit-2026-05-03.md, and docs/git-history-rewrite-plan.md. Two upstream repositories cloned into experiments/*/original/ at acquisition still hold their pre-redaction history and require their own equivalent passes against the same catalogue before any public-mirror cut-over.

Source: docs/redaction-policy.md; paper/main.md §5.6; rule 13 in CLAUDE.md.

The agent pipeline (stages 0–8)

The repository runs a ten-stage agent pipeline coordinated by an orchestrator. Executable prompts live under docs/prompts/. The orchestrator is the only agent permitted to launch other agents; every other agent runs in response to an orchestrator decision or an explicit human request.

0.

Orchestrator

Inspects state and dispatches the next stage. Records every decision in docs/handbacks/orchestrator-dispatch.md and docs/logbook.md. Prompt: orchestrator-prompt.md.

1.

Research protocol

New case study or evidence pass. Edits docs/sources.md, per-case provenance.md, RESEARCH-PROTOCOL.md. Prompt: research-protocol-prompt.md.

1.5.

Source analyzer

Upgrades [lit-retrieved] entries to [ai-confirmed] by retrieving and reading the source; flags edge cases. Prompt: source-analyzer-prompt.md.

2.

Scientific writer

Owns paper/main.{md,tex}, paper/main-condensed.{md,tex}, and paper/references.bib. Maintains rule-12 mirror discipline and rule-17 condensed-paper self-containment. Prompt: scientific-writer-prompt.md.

3.

Illustration

Owns paper/figures/*.{py,svg,pdf}. Pulls from [ai-confirmed] and [lit-read] data when generating data-driven figures. Prompt: illustration-prompt.md.

4.

Layout scrutinizer

Audits both PDFs against typographic / layout discipline; routes LAY-* and COND-LAY-* defects back to writer / illustrator. Prompt: layout-scrutinizer-prompt.md.

5.

Readability & novelty scrutinizer

Audits both Markdown sources for readability + novelty; routes RDB-* and COND-RDB-* defects. Prompt: readability-novelty-prompt.md.

6.

Aligner

End-to-end traceability audit (rule 18 + rule 19): paper ↔ sources ↔ figures ↔ README ↔ site. Routes ALN-* defects. Prompt: aligner-prompt.md.

7.

Modeler

Renders the traceability spine as a W3C PROV-O graph at docs/provenance.ttl. The browser-viewable rendering at graph.html is generated by scripts/build_provenance_site.py. Prompt: modeler-prompt.md.

8.

Site Agent

Owns docs/site/ — the deployed GitHub Pages surface (this site). Hand-authors index.html, paper.html, methodology.html, governance.html from canonical paper / README / sources / fair sources. Refuses to render without explicit publication consent (rule 14). Prompt: site-agent-prompt.md — introduced 2026-05-05.

Source: CLAUDE.md §Agent workflow; docs/human-ai-collaboration-process.md; the prompts under docs/prompts/.

How the eight integrated practices map to failure modes

  • Sloppification (proximate corruption of the scientific record by undisclosed AI-assisted output) — addressed by Practice 2 (verification-status labelling), Practice 1 (transcript preservation), Practice 6 (base-rate-anchored AI disclosure).
  • Model collapse (longer-horizon dilution of the corpus on which future foundation models train) — addressed by Practice 3 (provenance maps), Practice 4 (mirror discipline), Practice 8 (FAIR alignment / F(AI)²R).
  • Dual use (the asymmetry between integrator and adversary use of the same artifacts) — addressed by Practice 5 (recursive meta-process case study), the redaction discipline (precondition, not a numbered practice), and Practice 7 (legal honesty about authorship).

Source: paper/main.md §10; docs/human-ai-collaboration-process.md.
